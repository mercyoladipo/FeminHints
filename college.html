Pick the Right Time

  • If you schedule a visit during August or another time when the school is not in its normal session, you won’t be getting an accurate picture of what the school will be like in the fall.
  • The best time for a visit is during your Spring Break because oftentimes the breaks for college and high school students are in different weeks.

    • Meet with the Experts

  • If you’re serious about applying to the school, call ahead to schedule a meeting with the admissions officer. It’s a great way for them to put a face to your name when they come across your application!

    • Key Questions to ask yourself

  • How important is it to be at a culturally diverse school?
  • Large or small school?
  • Private school or Public school?
  • Location
  • 4-year or 2-year college/university?

    • Most importantly: Pick a school that is best for your major!

  • For example, a Fashion Design degree is less impressive from Harvard University than from Parsons.

    • Don't blow a task out of proportion

  • If tasks seem impossible, they’ll never get done! Split tasks up into manageable portions and prioritize them in order of importance.

    • Start with the hardest task of your day.

  • Once you complete your most daunting task, the rest will seem that much easier.
