      The phrase “going on a diet” is useless because, come on, the phrase contains “die”, meaning a “diet” will fail or “die” inevitably. So I'm gonna give you the overused expression that healthy eating isn't a diet, it's a lifestyle. But it really is.

      I mean 20/80 is totally true, but the real secret is that it's 100% mental. That's why I advise against saying “in _____ days I'll have the body I'll want”—nope, sorry hun that's not how it works. Or even saying “No sweets, no junk food, no soda, etc. until, I lose ____ pounds”—cold turkey, good one, but nice try...it's not going to work. (Unless you really are that good at cold turkey—teach me your secret—it's a plan for failure). Yes, in 90 days, heck maybe 60, good commitment of consistent well eating and exercise will pay off and change your body tremendously but it's just the beginning. If you really want that slim figure give it three years—the truth hurts. Now before you point fingers I want to address something: you are beautiful. You don't have to confine yourself to a number—I'm strictly speaking to those who want to change though, so don't think this is an attack on anyone. And yes, I get it, every body is different. I'm very well aware of this. There are some girls who can literally eat anything and lose weight (that's my brother) thanks to their killer metabolism. And then there are other girls who eat really well and may, even gain weight. It happens. I understand.
